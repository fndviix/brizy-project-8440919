Henry Lim Shien Ching FCA (Singapore), ASEAN CPA, CISA has more than 30 years of experience in both public and private sectors. He has vast experience in the field of external and internal audits, transaction support and tax compliance services.


Henry was awarded two Government scholarships to pursue his Bachelor (Honours) in Arts and Social Science degree with the National University of Singapore and then his Masters in Business Administration with Accountancy specialization degree with the Nanyang Business School.


He started his career with the Auditor-General Office raising to the rank of deputy director before leaving to join FKT – Grant Thornton and was rapidly admitted as an audit partner leading audits of both publicly listed companies and privately held enterprises. His experience includes audits of not-for-profit organisations including Government bodies and town councils. He also assisted companies seeking listing in the Singapore Stock Exchange as their reporting accountant as well as performed financial due diligence for companies as part of their mergers or acquisitions exercise.


In 2017, Henry joins us as a Partner to manage our Tax Department in addition to providing advisory services. He also acts as our Technical Partner on accounting and taxation matters. Henry is passionate about nurturing a younger generation of accountants and is actively involved in imparting his skills and knowledge to the younger generation through his involvement with the Singapore CA Qualification as a workshop facilitator and examination marker. He is also an adjunct lecturer at SUSS.

Rohan Kamis FCA FCPA FCCA, ASEAN CPA, formed Rohan · Mah & Partners LLP in October 1993 to service the niche between the Big Four international accounting firms and the smaller local firms.


He graduated in 1975 from the University of Singapore in Accountancy. He was the PAP Member of Parliament (MP) for Telok Blangah Constituency from February 1979 to December 1984. Whilst as an MP, he was a member of the Parliamentary Public Accounts Committee that was responsible for the statutory and value-for-money audits for all Government Ministries, Statutory Boards and Government Companies. He is also a member of several professional institutions including the British Computer Society.


Rohan held many important portfolios in several quasi-government, commercial and professional organizations. In 1987 he set up Rohan + Siew, an accounting practice with management consultancy services specializing in Executive Search & Selection before forming Rohan · Mah & Partners LLP. He is also the founding Chairman of ASNAF Public Accounting Corporation.


He sits on the boards of several private and public companies. In addition, he is on the Inquiry and Disciplinary panels of the Singapore Medical Council, the Law Society and the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority. In July 2014, he co-edited and launched a book of autobiographical essays “We also SERVED: Reflections of Singapore former PAP MPs".


As Managing Partner, he is to ensure the continued growth and profitability of the firm.

Suree Rohan CA, ASEAN CPA, graduated in 2001 with a Bachelor (Honours) of Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Nanyang Technological University in Singapore.


Upon graduation, she started her career in wealth management with Citibank, an American multi-national banking and financial services corporation.


She then went on to attain her Masters in Business Administration with a specialisation in Accountancy from Nanyang Business School in Singapore.


Her accountancy career started with an international accounting firm, Ernst & Young LLP as an auditor for international financial institutions such as banks, hedge funds, asset managers and global commodity trading companies. Thereafter, she took on an Advisory role as a Regional Consultant with financial institutions in Asia Pacific. Her risk management, internal audit, compliance and corporate governance work saw her based in China, Hong Kong, Thailand, Vietnam and Malaysia.


Suree is a Principal in RMP and heads the Advisory Department for financial institutions. She is currently a member of the Board of PPIS, a Voluntary Welfare Organisation in Singapore where she chaired their Audit Advisory Committee from 2015-2019. She currently is the Co-Chairperson for both the PPIS Children Services Advisory Committee and PPIS Fund Raising Advisory Committee. She is also a member of the Community Chest Committee, the fund-raising arm of the National Council of Social Service. And on the Arts Consultative Panel of the Info-communications Media Development Authority of Singapore (IMDA).


She loves travelling at the spur of the moment but not the packing.

Tan How Choon FCA (Singapore) has about 40 years of experience in forensic accounting and IT services with both public and private sectors. He is a Certified IT/S Auditor, Adjunct Associate Professor of NTU and a Training Consultant and Secretary of White Collar Crime Investigation Program/Committee under Prime Minister’s Office strategic leadership initiative.


How Choon was Chief Information Officer with Ministry of Home Affairs. His previous job holdings included Senior Systems Consultant at Sperry Rand/Sperry Computer Systems (now Unisys), Chief IT Auditor and Director of Government Information Services Department at Singapore National Computer Board.


At NCB, he was involved in several national strategic planning projects including IT2000/Intelligent Island, Library 2000, Civil Service Computerized Systems (CSCP). He led the Ministry of Home Affairs’ National Registration Identity Card project team to successfully implement the New NRIC System and Central Investigation Department’s Finger Print System in 1990. In late 1980s, he designed and implemented the first Enterprise Resource Planning System for the German Rollei Camera factory in Singapore before ERP became fashionable.


He graduated from Nanyang University with a B. Com. degree and a Post Graduate Diploma in Computer Science. Subsequently, he pursued and obtained MSc (Management of Technology) (NUS) and MBA (Accountancy) (NTU) whilst achieving scholarships from National Computer Board. He was also Adjunct Associate Professor with National University of Singapore (NUS) Business School’s MBA program from 1998 to 2004 and Australian National University (ANU)’s Master of International Management program from 2001 to 2004.


How Choon joins RMP as the Director, Forensic & IT Services and expert witness for related court cases.
