THE FINANCIAL YEAR 2020 BUDGET

The Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Finance, Mr. Heng Swee Keat, announced the 2020 Budget in Parliament on 18 February 2020. Highlights of the tax and other changes are as follows:

1. Individual Tax Changes
  • Withholding tax exemption for Non-resident Mediators for mediation work carried out in Singapore will be extended till 31 March 2022.
  • Withholding tax exemption for Non-resident Arbitrators for arbitration work carried out in Singapore will be extended till 31 March 2022.
  • Withholding tax rate for Non-resident Public Entertainers will be reduced from 15% to 10% for services performed in Singapore and the exemption will be extended till 31 March 2022.  
2. Corporate/Business Tax Changes
  • 25% Corporate Income Tax (“CIT”) rebate will be given to all companies, subject to cap of S$15,000 for the YA 2020.
  • Additional 2 months of interest-free instalment payment will be given to companies under the GIRO Scheme.
  • The Carry-back Relief Scheme will allow taxpayers to carry back qualifying deductions for YA 2020 up to 3 immediate preceding YAs.
  • For Plant and Machinery (“P&M”) acquired in the basis period for YA2021, taxpayers will have the option to accelerate the write-off of the cost over 2 years.
  • For qualifying Renovation and Refurbishment (“R&R”) expenditure incurred during the basis period for YA2021, the taxpayer will have the option to claim R&R deduction over 1 YA.
  • The Double Tax Deduction for Internationalisation (“DTDi”) scheme will be extended till 31 December 2025.
  • The Mergers and Acquisitions (“M&A”) scheme will be extended till 31 December 2025.
  • The Maritime Sector Incentive (“MSI”) will be extended till 31 December 2026.
  • The Finance and Treasury Centre (“FTC”) scheme will be extended till 31 December 2026.
  • The Global Trader Programme (“GTP”) will be extended till 31 December 2026.
  • The Section 13H Scheme for Venture Capital Funds and the Fund Management Incentive will be extended till 31 December 2025.
  • The Land Intensification Allowance (“LIA”) scheme will be extended till 31 December 2025.
  • The Writing -down Value (“WDA”) scheme will be extended till 31 December 2025.
  • Businesses may opt to write down their P&M based on the new distributed Working Life of P&M for CA Claims starting from FY 2022.
  • Recipients will not be allowed to claim tax deductions or allowances on that part of the expenditures that are funded by such grants from the Government or statutory board for capital grants approved on or after 1 January 2021.
3. Goods and Services Tax ("GST") Changes
  • GST rate remains at 7% in 2021.
4. Other Changes
  • With effective from 1 January 2021 to 31 December 2022, the Senior Employment Credit (“SEC”) scheme provides wage offsets of up to 8%, depending on the age of the employee, to those employing Singaporeans aged 55 and above and earning up to S$4,000 a month.
  • From 1 January 2021 to 31 December 2021, the Central Provident Fund (“CPF”) Transition Offset will be given to employers of Singaporean and Permanent Resident workers aged above 55 to 70 to offset half of the increase in employer CPF contribution rate.
  • Enterprises will receive an 8% cash grant on the gross monthly wages of each local employee on their CPF payroll for the months of October 2019 to December 2019, subject to a monthly wage cap of S$3,600 per employee under the Jobs Support Scheme (“JSS”) by 31 July 2020.
  • The Enhancement to Wage Credit Scheme (“WCS”) will be enhanced to continue to encourage employers to share productivity gains with workers.
  • Qualifying commercial properties will be granted a rebate for Property Tax (“PT”) payable for the period 1 January 2020 to 31 December 2020.
  • With effective from March 2020, eligible tourism sector enterprises can borrow up to S$1 million, with the interest rate capped at 5% p.a., from Participating Financial Institutions. The Government will provide 80% risk-share on these loans under the Temporary Bridging Loan Programme (“TBLP”) till March 2021.
  • The Aviation Sector Assistance Package (“ASAP”) will be provided with Landing Charge rebates, credits and other assistance during the COVID-19 outbreak period.
  • The Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore (“MPA”) will be giving 50% port dues concession to cruise ships and regional ferries with a port stay of not more than five days, and passenger-carrying harbor craft for the period 1 March 2020 to 1 August 2020.
  • Rental Waivers will be provided to qualifying Commercial Tenants in Government-owned / managed Facilities.
  • The Enhancement to the Enterprise Financing Scheme (“EFS”) managed by Enterprise Singapore (“ESG”) will share the loan default risk in the event of enterprise insolvency with the Participating Financial Institutions for the qualifying types of loans.
  • An Enterprise Transform Package aims at grooming business leaders of promising SMEs over the next 3 years through training and mentorship, in collaboration with institutes of higher learning was introduced.
  • The SMEs Go Digital Scheme that helps SMEs select digital solutions and skills training programmes relevant to them will be expanded to other Industry Transformation Maps (“ITMs”).
  • Eligible SMEs will receive up to 70% of eligible costs, capped at S$100,000 per new market per company in the enhancement period of FY2020 to 2022 to cover supportable activities such as promotion of products and services overseas and identification of business partners under the Market Readiness Assistance (“MRA”).
